01 — Problem & Goals

The Gap Between
Brand & Experience

Maison Éclat was a beloved high-end furniture brand with loyal showroom clients — but their e-commerce site told a completely different story. Cart abandonment sat at 71%, and the product experience was generic enough to belong to any budget retailer.

My mandate: redesign product discovery and checkout to match the brand's luxury positioning. As an interior designer, I understood exactly what this audience needed — and what they'd reject.

71%
Cart Abandonment
Most users dropped off before purchasing — often at the product page stage.
4.2s
Avg. Time on PDP
Product pages weren't holding attention — users weren't getting what they needed to commit.
0%
Room Visualizer Use
An existing AR room tool was buried so deep that virtually no users ever found it.

02 — Research & Discovery

What Luxury Shoppers
Actually Need

I analyzed Hotjar heatmaps and session recordings, then conducted 12 interviews with high-intent luxury furniture shoppers. The pattern was clear: they needed to be seduced before they wanted specs.

"When I'm spending this much on a sofa, I need to see it in a room that looks like mine. And I need to feel like the brand understands taste — not just products."

— Interview participant, 44, homeowner

01
Inspire First
Lead with editorial photography and room context before product specs enter the picture.
02
Inform Second
Material, provenance, and craftsmanship details build conviction — once the user is emotionally engaged.
03
Transact Third
The checkout is a commitment ceremony, not a form. Make it feel effortless and reassuring.

Key insight from competitive benchmarking: luxury buyers are conviction shoppers, not comparison shoppers. The job of the UX is to build conviction, not optimize for speed.

03 — Process & Wireframes

Designing for
Conviction

I restructured the entire site around the principle: inspire first, inform second, transact third. Every screen was evaluated against this order — if it didn't inspire first, it didn't belong at the top of the experience.

05 — Results & Impact

Where Luxury Meets
Conversion

Three months post-launch, the redesign delivered improvement across every tracked metric — and qualitative signals suggested an equally significant brand perception shift.

+23%
Conversion Rate
Overall e-commerce conversion improved from 1.4% to 1.72% — significant at Maison Éclat's average order value.
−41%
Cart Abandonment
Abandonment fell from 71% to 42% following the checkout redesign and trust messaging improvements.
3.1×
Room Visualizer Use
Surfacing the visualizer as a primary CTA increased usage by 310% — and users who used it converted at 2× baseline.
+18%
Average Order Value
Editorial "Complete the Room" modules lifted AOV by 18% as users discovered complementary pieces.
