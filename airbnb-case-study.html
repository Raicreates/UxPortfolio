01 — Problem & Goals

A Feature With
Untapped Potential

Airbnb's Wishlist lets users save listings they love. On paper, it's a powerful trip-planning tool. In practice, it was an afterthought — a folder most users filled with unsorted listings they'd never revisit.

The friction was real: no way to organize by trip, no collaborative editing for group travel, no contextual notes, and no connection to actual availability. Trip planning is inherently social, but the Wishlist was built for individuals.

63%
Wishlist Abandonment
Most wishlists are never converted into bookings — users lose track and context.
41%
Group Travel Share
Over 40% of Airbnb stays involve groups, yet the Wishlist had no collaborative features.
1 list
Avg. Lists Per User
Most users maintained one disorganized list — a signal the feature wasn't meeting their mental model.

02 — Research & Discovery

How People Actually
Plan Trips Together

I interviewed 15 Airbnb users across different travel patterns: solo travelers, couples, and group trip organizers. The group organizer interviews were the most revealing — a consistently chaotic multi-platform process involving shared Google Docs, WhatsApp threads, and screenshot storms.

03 — Process & Wireframes

Building a Planning
Tool, Not a Folder

I reframed the Wishlist as a "Trip Board" — a collaborative, trip-specific planning space with real-world context baked in from the moment of saving. The mental model shifted from "save for later" to "plan together now."

01
Trip-Centric Structure
Users create Trip Boards tied to specific destinations and dates. Availability is checked automatically — stale listings are flagged, not silently outdated.
02
Collaborative Voting
Invited collaborators react to listings with Love it / It's okay / Not for me. A "group vibe" score aggregates reactions to surface consensus without a group chat.
03
Save with Context
A 2-second micro-flow when saving lets users add a quick note and tag the trip — dramatically increasing the usefulness of the saved item later.
04
Compare & Decide View
A side-by-side comparison mode lets users evaluate 2–3 properties across key criteria without toggling between listings.
04 — Final Design

Trip Planning as a
Native Experience

The final design transformed the Wishlist from a passive collection into an active planning environment. My interior design background shaped the compare view in particular — I drew on how designers present options to clients: side-by-side, with the right detail, at the right moment.

05 — Results & Impact

Tested, Validated,
Ready to Ship

Results were measured through moderated usability testing with 10 participants using a high-fidelity Figma prototype. Metrics focused on task completion and desirability.

94%
Task Completion
Participants created a Trip Board, invited a collaborator, and reached a voting outcome in under 4 minutes.
9.1/10
Desirability Score
"How much would you want this feature?" — average 9.1, with group travelers scoring it 9.6.
−60%
Coordination Steps
Participants estimated the concept would eliminate ~60% of external coordination from their current group planning process.
100%
Would Use It
Every participant said they'd use Trip Boards on their next group trip if available today.
