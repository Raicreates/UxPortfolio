01 — Problem & Goals

A Powerful App That
Nobody Could Finish

RoomCraft had impressive 3D rendering technology under the hood — but a 38% drop-off rate during onboarding told a different story. Users were abandoning before ever placing a single piece of furniture.

As someone with an interior design background, I immediately recognized the disconnect: the app was designed by engineers who understood rendering, not by people who understand how humans think about rooms. People don't start with technical parameters — they start with a feeling.

02 — Research & Discovery

Listening Before
Drawing Anything

I conducted 18 moderated user interviews across three segments: first-time renters, homeowners redecorating, and interior design enthusiasts. The most revealing insight: people don't start with dimensions — they start with inspiration.

Finding 01
Inspiration First
Users start planning with images, moods, and references — not dimensions. The app forced them to work backwards.
Finding 02
Blank Canvas Fear
An empty room created decision paralysis. Users needed something to react to, not build from scratch.
Finding 03
Form Fatigue
The 11-field room setup form triggered immediate overwhelm. Users wanted to "just try it" first.
Finding 04
Emotional Decisions
74% of room planning decisions are emotionally driven. The technical-first approach was fundamentally misaligned.

"I open Pinterest first. I collect images until I know the vibe. This app made me do it completely backwards."

— Interview participant, 29, first-time renter

05 — Results & Impact

Numbers That
Tell the Story

Six weeks after launch, the redesigned onboarding showed significant improvements across every key metric. Users stopped describing RoomCraft as "complicated" and started calling it "fun."

−38%
Drop-off Rate Reduced
Completion jumped from 62% to 85% within the first month post-launch.
+52%
Session Length
Average session duration increased from 6 to over 9 minutes.
4.6★
App Store Rating
Rating climbed from 2.1 to 4.6 stars in 6 weeks post-launch.
+28%
7-Day Retention
Users who completed new onboarding returned significantly more often.
